Draw Cheque for Cash

About the fields
Bank Account

The bank account the cheque is being raised against and money is being withdrawn from.

Amount

The full value of the cheque.

Date

The cheque date.

Cheque No

The number on the cheque you are taking to be cashed.

Tip: You might need to amend this number if the cheque you are entering is to correct an error on a previous paying-in slip or on a draw cheque for cash transaction. If so, we recommend that you use a cheque number outside your current range, e.g. 00001. Try not to make it a larger number than the one you are using currently.

Note: If Fund Manager won’t accept your cheque number, this may be because they are duplicated. For information on how to resolve this, see How do I deal with a duplicated cheque?

Notes

Notes which help you identify the cheque you are cashing in.

This is where you record cheques which you want to cash.

How do I ...

Record details of the cheque?
  1. Select the bank account.
  2. Enter the cheque value as Amount.
  3. The date defaults to today’s date. Amend the date if required to that on the cheque.
  4. Enter the actual cheque number which is on the cheque by overwriting the automatically generated cheque number.
  5. Enter any notes that will help you to identify the cheque you are cashing.
  6. Click Draw Cheque.
Correct the value of a cashed cheque?

If the amount you cashed was too low, then draw another cheque.

Tip: Enter a cheque number that is unique but outside your current range, e.g. 00001. Try not to make it a larger number than the one you are using currently.

If the amount you cashed was too high, you will need to pay the excess back into the bank, using Bank & Cash > Pay Receipts into Bank.

Cancel a cheque I have drawn for cash?
  • You will need to pay the cash back into the bank, using Bank & Cash > Pay Receipts to Bank.
